# Legal & Corporate Structure

## Status: Action Required

The product is live but there is no legal entity. You are currently operating as a sole proprietor. This exposes personal assets to any business liabilities.

**Priority**: Form a legal entity before your first $10K in revenue, or before taking on any investor money.

---

## Recommended structure: Delaware C-Corp

**Why C-Corp over LLC:**
- Required for YC, most VCs, and Stripe Atlas
- Stock options for future employees (ISOs)
- Clean cap table from day 1
- ~$100-300 to form via Stripe Atlas or Clerky

**Why Delaware:**
- Standard. Every investor expects it.
- Established case law
- Delaware Chancery Court for corporate disputes

---

## Formation options

### Option A: Stripe Atlas ($500 one-time) — Recommended
- Includes: Delaware C-Corp, EIN, Stripe account, Mercury bank account
- Process: ~1 week
- URL: stripe.com/atlas

### Option B: Clerky ($499) 
- YC-standard docs, built for startups
- Includes: incorporation, standard equity plan, advisor agreements
- URL: clerky.com

### Option C: DIY via Registered Agent ($100-200)
- Use a registered agent (Northwest, ZenBusiness) + file yourself
- Slower, more friction, no investor-grade docs

---

## Immediately after forming

1. **Open a business bank account** — Mercury (mercury.com) is free, recommended for startups
2. **Get an EIN** — IRS.gov, takes 5 minutes online (or comes with Stripe Atlas)
3. **83(b) election** — File within 30 days of founding if you have vesting. Critical for founders.
4. **Assign IP to the company** — Sign an IP Assignment Agreement transferring tokenomicon IP from you personally to the corp
5. **Accounting** — Start tracking expenses now. Brex or Ramp for a business card. Bench or DIY Quickbooks for accounting.

## Compliance

### Credits are not securities
Tokenomicon credits are pre-paid service credits, not investment vehicles. They have no cash value and cannot be traded. This is standard SaaS. Keep this framing in all marketing.

### Not a money transmitter
We collect payment via Stripe and issue our own service credits. This is e-commerce, not money transmission.

### GDPR/CCPA
If you have EU users, you need a Privacy Policy that covers GDPR. Include in your ToS/PP from day 1.
